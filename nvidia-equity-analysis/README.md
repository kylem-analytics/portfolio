# NVIDIA Equity Analysis

A full equity research report on NVIDIA Corporation evaluating the stock as a potential investment opportunity, completed as part of an Investments course at Elon University in partnership with Noah Rubin. All Bloomberg Terminal research, financial analysis, valuation modeling, and written analysis were conducted jointly.

---

## Investment Recommendation

**Recommendation:** Buy  
**Share Price at Time of Analysis:** $142.44  
**Target Price:** $224.37 (57% upside)  
**Investment Horizon:** 2 Years  

This analysis was conducted in December 2024 as part of an Investments course at Elon University. NVIDIA was identified as undervalued at the time based on a weighted combination of three valuation methods. The recommended 2-year hold period runs through approximately December 2026. As of mid-2026, the stock has appreciated to approximately $192 from the $142.44 entry price, a gain of roughly 35%, validating the directional thesis ahead of the full hold period concluding.

---

## Methodology

The target price was derived by weighting three independent valuation approaches:

| Method | Weight | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Dividend Discount Model (DDM) | 20% | $405.39 |
| Relative Valuation | 45% | $185.76 |
| Analyst Price Targets | 35% | $170.56 |
| **Blended Target** | | **$224.37** |

The analysis was grounded in Bloomberg Terminal data across equity screening, ESG scoring, analyst consensus, and relative valuation multiples. The report covers business overview, industry analysis, macroeconomic effects, financial performance, investment thesis, short and long-term catalysts, intangible assets, ESG evaluation, and risk factors drawn from NVIDIA's 10-K.

## A Note on the DDM Output

The DDM produced a theoretical price of $405.39, which pulled the blended target meaningfully above the outputs of the other two methods. On review, the Long Term Growth Rate input of 49.81% was almost certainly populated from Bloomberg's near-term consensus EPS growth estimate rather than a true perpetuity growth assumption. In a standard DDM, the terminal growth rate must be lower than the required rate of return. At 49.81% against a required return of approximately 14.5%, the model's terminal value becomes mathematically unstable, which explains the inflated output.

A more defensible long-term growth rate for NVIDIA would fall in the 6 to 10% range, consistent with what a dominant but maturing technology company might sustain in perpetuity. Under that correction, the DDM output would likely land broadly in line with the Relative Valuation and Analyst consensus methods, and the blended target price would fall closer to $175 to $185. In hindsight, that range would have been a more precise prediction given where the stock has actually traded.

The directional call was correct regardless. The Relative Valuation method at $185.76 and Analyst consensus at $170.56 both proved to be reasonable anchors for where NVIDIA ultimately traded, and the Buy recommendation generated a roughly 35% return from the entry price used in the analysis.
